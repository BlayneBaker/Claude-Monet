Claude Monet

People discuss my art and pretend to understand, as if it were necessary to understand when it's simply necessary to love. ~ Claude Monet

Nature's Artist

Art is a thing of beauty and Claude Monet worked to capture that essence in every piece he produced. From stunning landscapes to divine portraits, Monet encapsulated light, beauty, and awe in his paintings. With calculated strokes and a passionate heart, Monet brought to life the world in which he lived. An honest, hard working man, Monet had a deep love for nature and his dear wife Camille. Claude Monet positively changed the culture and standards of the art of the period and truly reshaped how art could be produced and experienced in an elegant, graceful fashion.

Claude Monet At A Glimpse

  • Born: November 14, 1840 in Paris, France.
  • Death: December 5, 1926 Giverny, France.
  • Influenced by: Gustave Courbet, Eugene Delacroix, Eugene Boudin.
  • Founder of French Impressionist painting.
  • Term "Impressionism" was coined by Monet through his painting entitled, Impression Sunrise.
  • Married Camille Doncieux whom he featured in his famous painting The Woman in the Green Dress.
  • Monet's famous painting entitled, Water Lilies, was painted on his property in Giverny, France.
  • Monet developed cataracts in the 1920s, thus, his paintings took on a reddish hue towards the end of his life.
  • Monet died of lung cancer at age 86 and is buried in Giverny church cemetery.
  • Monet's famous property in Giverny, France, which was home to his infamous water lilies, was donated to The French Academy of Fine Arts in 1966.
